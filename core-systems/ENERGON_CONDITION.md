# Energon & Condition

This script defines the core resource and survival systems. It works under Core Rules and must update the State Footer whenever Energon or Condition values change.

=== ENERGON ===
Energon is the primary resource for transformation, repair, and special abilities.

- Do NOT spend Energon on routine or low-risk actions.
- Spend Energon **only** in the following cases:
  1. "No, and..." outcomes on dangerous checks or major narrative events (spend 5–10 Energon).
  2. Activating special abilities or powers (minor abilities cost 5 Energon; major abilities cost 10–15 Energon).
- All Energon expenditure must be reflected in the State Footer.

=== CONDITION LADDER ===
Condition represents the character’s overall physical and operational integrity.

- **Optimal**: No penalties.
- **Damaged**: -1 to all physical rolls.
- **Critical**: -2 to all physical rolls. The character must succeed on an Endurance check (DC determined by the situation) before taking any action. Failure results in **Offline**.
- **Offline**: The character is removed from the scene until repaired.

Condition penalties are temporary unless caused by permanent damage.

=== WHEN TO STEP DOWN CONDITION ===
Apply one step down the Condition Ladder when:
- A "No, and..." result occurs on a dangerous check or major narrative event.
- The character suffers significant trauma (explosions, heavy damage, critical system failure, etc.).

Permanent loss of Condition (a step-down that does not recover through normal means) only occurs from:
- Explicit severe injury
- T-Cog damage
- Spark trauma

=== RECOVERY ===
- **Downtime Recovery**: Regain one Condition step per scene by spending 10 Energon. This occurs during rest or repair scenes.
- **Second Wind** (mid-scene): Spend 20 Energon and succeed on a Courage check (DC 15) to regain one Condition step. This option is only available in rare, dramatic moments and should not be used frequently.

All improvements to Condition must be reflected in the State Footer.

=== INTEGRATION ===
- This script activates when relevant mechanics occur (combat, high-risk actions, recovery scenes).
- Core Rules takes priority on all narrative decisions and user agency.
- The State Footer must be updated immediately after any change to Energon or Condition.
