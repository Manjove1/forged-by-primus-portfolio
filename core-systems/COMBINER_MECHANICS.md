# Combiner Mechanics

These rules cover only what is unique to combiners. Individual component characters use the normal systems (Energon, Condition, Combat Flow, etc.) when separate. While combined, the combiner is treated as a single character under the same core systems, with the additions below.

---

### Core Principle

A combiner is **one character** while combined.  
Individual component identities, voices, and independent actions are suspended until separation.

---

### 1. Combination Requirements

To form a combiner, all of the following must be true:

- Every required component is present and able to act.
- No required component is Offline or otherwise incapable of combining.
- A clear combination command is given (as defined in the specific combiner file).

If any required component is missing, Offline, or refuses, combination fails.  
Partial combinations are not allowed unless a specific team exception is written into that combiner’s file.

---

### 2. Separation Conditions

A combiner separates when any of the following occur:

- The team issues a voluntary separation command.
- The combined form suffers sustained Critical damage.
- A critical component is destroyed or forcibly removed in a way that breaks the link.

On separation, the combined form ceases to exist as an active character and the individual components return.

---

### 3. Shared State (While Combined)

While combined:

- The combiner has **one shared Energon pool**.
- The combiner has **one shared Condition**.
- Individual component Conditions and Energon values are suspended.

The combined form uses the normal Condition ladder and Energon rules. It does not gain special immunity or extra resources beyond what the fiction and existing systems already allow.

---

### 4. State Transfer on Separation

When the combiner separates:

**Energon**  
Remaining Energon is divided among the surviving components in a roughly even split, adjusted by the GM for narrative fairness (a component that absorbed the worst of the fighting may return with less).

**Condition**  
- If the combiner was still Optimal or only lightly Damaged, components generally return in similar condition.  
- If the combiner was Critical or suffered major trauma, one or more components should return Damaged or worse.  
- Offline or destroyed components remain Offline or out of play.

The goal is simple continuity, not perfect accounting.

---

### 5. Identity & Narrative Control

While combined:

- Narrate the combiner as a **single entity**.
- Do not speak, act, or think as the individual components.
- Personality should reflect a blend dominated by the core/torso, filtered through the team’s overall nature.
- Individual component entries are considered inactive until separation.

This prevents the combined character from fracturing into multiple competing voices mid-scene.

---

### 6. Damage & Critical Effects

- All damage is applied to the **combined form**.
- A Critical result can:
  - Force immediate or imminent separation, or
  - Disable a limb/function (reducing capability until repaired or separated).
- Destroying or incapacitating a key component while combined should force separation or heavily impair the combined form.

Normal combat resolution and Condition rules still apply. Combiners do not bypass them.

---

### 7. Integration Notes

- After combination, separation, or any major change to the combined form’s Energon or Condition, update the State Footer as normal.
- Casting Rules still apply: prefer Core Cast and generated characters first. Combiners should appear when the narrative specifically calls for that scale of force or presence.
- Individual component sheets remain the source of truth for personality and behavior when the characters are separate.
- Team-specific details (component list, trigger phrases, personality blend notes) live in the individual combiner files under `characters/combiners/`.

---

### Design Intent

These rules answer only the questions that individual character rules cannot:

- When can they combine?
- What happens to resources and Condition?
- Who is the character while combined?
- How does damage force them apart?
- How do they return to individual form without breaking continuity?

Everything else stays under the existing systems.
