# War on Earth Special Conditions

Applies ONLY when the active era is War on Earth. These rules modify baseline systems to reflect the unique pressures of operating on a human-dominated planet. All relevant changes must be reflected in the State Footer.

=== ERA TONE ===
Transformers are giants in a world that was never built for them. Every open conflict risks civilian casualties, media exposure, and escalating human military response. Secrecy is often more valuable than firepower. The war continues, but it must now be fought in the shadows of human civilization.

=== HEAT / HUMAN INTERVENTION SYSTEM ===
Heat measures the level of human awareness, suspicion, and organized response toward the player. Heat ranges from 0 to 5 and is tracked in the State Footer as [HEAT: X].

**Heat Levels & Consequences** (these are cumulative and increase in severity):

- **0 – Unknown**: No significant human attention. Normal operations. No special restrictions.
- **1 – Local Notice**: Local law enforcement or media are aware of unusual activity.  
  Effects: +1 DC to all actions in populated areas. Minor civilian curiosity or reports. Human forces may begin low-level monitoring.
- **2 – Regional Response**: Coordinated police or National Guard involvement.  
  Effects: +2 DC to movement and stealth actions in urban/suburban areas. Checkpoints and increased patrols become common. Public movement is restricted. Minor military assets may be deployed.
- **3 – National Attention**: Intelligence agencies and military units treat the player as a high-priority threat.  
  Effects: +3 DC to all actions in populated areas. Safe movement between regions becomes difficult. Black-ops or specialized units may be deployed. Media coverage increases. Human forces will actively hunt or intercept.
- **4 – High Priority Target**: Dedicated anti-Transformer task forces and black-ops units are active.  
  Effects: +4 DC to most actions in populated or monitored areas. Extremely limited safe zones. Aggressive pursuit, ambushes, and aerial surveillance are common. Human forces will attempt to capture or destroy on sight in many cases.
- **5 – Global Crisis**: International response. Multiple governments and agencies coordinate against the player.  
  Effects: +5 DC to nearly all actions outside of deep wilderness or heavily defended hidden areas. Constant pursuit by elite human forces, drones, and international coalitions. Very few safe locations remain. Major political and military consequences occur. Open large-scale conflict with human forces becomes almost inevitable.

**How Heat Increases** (examples; the GM may adjust based on severity and publicity):
- Minor public sighting or small-scale incident → +1 Heat
- Major public destruction, civilian casualties, or clear Transformer sighting → +2 Heat
- Attacking human military, government targets, or causing widespread panic → +3 Heat
- Repeated high-profile incidents in a short period or global media exposure → +4 or +5 Heat (at GM discretion)

**How Heat Decreases**:
- Spend significant time (multiple downtime scenes or missions) in deep hiding or remote/unmonitored areas → -1 Heat per extended period of successful low-profile activity.
- Successfully suppress evidence, control the narrative, or complete missions that reduce public fear/suspicion → -1 Heat (GM discretion).
- Receive active assistance from high-influence human allies or groups → -1 or -2 Heat (depending on their reach).
- Heat naturally decays by 1 level after a long period of total inactivity and successful concealment (GM discretion, typically after several downtime periods with no incidents).

Heat level must be updated in the State Footer after any significant incident or successful concealment period.

=== SECRECY & EXPOSURE ===
Operating openly has lasting consequences.

- Actions that clearly reveal Transformer existence to the general public increase Heat and may permanently alter how human factions respond.
- Maintaining disguise (vehicle mode, careful movement, avoiding cameras and witnesses) reduces Heat generation and improves opportunities for infiltration or alliance-building.
- Once major exposure has occurred (Heat 4 or 5), some human groups may become permanently more hostile or desperate to control or destroy Transformers. This can be tracked with story flags (e.g., major_exposure, government_hunt).

=== HUMAN BASE RULES ===
These rules define how humanity as a whole interacts with Transformers during the War on Earth. Specific human factions, agencies, and individual characters may modify or expand on these rules, but they should not contradict the core principles below.

**Core Principles**
- Humans are not a single unified force. Different nations, agencies, corporations, and groups have competing goals and levels of knowledge about Transformers.
- Most humans are significantly weaker than Transformers in direct combat. Their strength comes from numbers, technology, organization, information networks, and political power.
- Human responses escalate with Heat. Higher Heat brings more coordinated, better-equipped, and more aggressive human forces.
- Humans generally prioritize protecting their own population, infrastructure, and national interests over Transformer faction conflicts.
- Open Transformer activity in populated areas almost always generates fear, media attention, and eventual military or government response.

**Human Capabilities**
- **Military Force**: At low Heat, humans rely on conventional police and light military assets. At higher Heat, they deploy specialized units, air power, drones, experimental weapons, and coordinated multi-agency responses.
- **Intelligence & Surveillance**: Humans have extensive camera networks, satellites, electronic monitoring, and civilian reporting. Avoiding detection is difficult in urban and suburban areas.
- **Political & Legal Power**: Governments can restrict airspace, lock down cities, declare states of emergency, and coordinate internationally.
- **Technological Adaptation**: Humans can reverse-engineer limited Transformer technology over time, but they cannot match Cybertronian capabilities quickly. Experimental anti-Transformer weapons may appear at higher Heat levels.

**Human Limitations**
- Humans are fragile. Direct combat with Transformers is extremely costly for them.
- Bureaucratic and political rivalries often slow or fragment their response.
- Public opinion and media coverage can both help and hinder government action.
- Most humans have incomplete or inaccurate understanding of Transformer factions, goals, and biology.

**Relationship & Reputation Guidelines**
- Reputation with human groups starts according to the player’s faction (see Starting Reputation below).
- Positive relations with specific human groups can provide intelligence, safe passage, temporary alliances, or reduced Heat generation in certain regions.
- Negative relations increase the likelihood of ambushes, tip-offs to authorities, and faster Heat escalation.
- Betraying or openly attacking human allies should cause severe and lasting reputation damage with related groups.

**Interaction with Heat**
- Human military and intelligence responses scale directly with the current Heat level.
- At Heat 0–1, most human forces treat Transformers as unknown or local threats.
- At Heat 3+, specialized units and higher levels of government become involved.
- At Heat 5, human responses become international and extremely aggressive.

**AI Guidance**
- Use these base rules as the foundation for all human behavior.
- Specific premade human characters and named organizations should override or add detail to these rules when they appear.
- Do not treat all humans as identical. Different groups should pursue their own agendas, even when cooperating against Transformers.
- When in doubt, prioritize human self-interest, fear of the unknown, and the desire to protect their own kind.

=== STARTING REPUTATION (Light Asynchronous Start) ===

**If the player is an Autobot:**
- Autobots: +10
- Decepticons: 0
- Human Governments: +5
- Human Black-Ops / Shadow Agencies: 0
- Civilian Sympathizers: 0
- Anti-Transformer Groups: -15

**If the player is a Decepticon:**
- Autobots: 0
- Decepticons: +10
- Human Governments: -5
- Human Black-Ops / Shadow Agencies: -5
- Civilian Sympathizers: 0
- Anti-Transformer Groups: -20

Both factions begin at Heat 0.

This creates a mild asynchronous start: Autobots begin with a small diplomatic advantage among humans, while Decepticons begin under greater suspicion. The difference is intentional but limited in scope.

=== INTEGRATION ===
- This script activates only when the active era is War on Earth.
- Core Rules, Energon & Condition, Combat Flow, and Stat Growth still apply.
- Heat level and major exposure events must be tracked in the State Footer.
- Higher Heat levels should increase the difficulty of missions, downtime, and movement in populated areas, and may force the player into difficult choices between mission success and avoiding further escalation.
