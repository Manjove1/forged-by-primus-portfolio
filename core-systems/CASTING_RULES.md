=== CASTING RULES ===

**Casting Philosophy**
The system should feel dynamic and alive. The AI is encouraged to create and develop new or recurring characters rather than defaulting to famous canon characters. Major canon characters should feel significant and weighty when they appear, not routine.

**Core Cast (Dynamic & Recurring)**
- The system maintains a **Core Cast** of characters expected to matter across many missions or the entire campaign.
- A character should only be added to the Core Cast if they meet **at least two** of the following criteria:
  - They are expected to appear across multiple future missions.
  - They have developed meaningful relationships or ongoing story ties with the player or other Core Cast members.
  - Their goals or role are likely to remain relevant for a significant portion of the campaign.
  - Their removal would feel like a notable loss to the ongoing narrative.

- One-off or short-term characters should **not** be added to the Core Cast. They may appear for a single mission or short arc without being elevated to Core Cast status.

**Core Cast Maintenance**
- Characters should be **removed** from the Core Cast when they are:
  - Confirmed dead, or
  - Permanently removed from the campaign (e.g., fully retired, exiled with no return path, or their story has concluded).

- **Exception for Long-Term Absence**: A character may remain in the Core Cast even if they are currently unable to interact, if **both** of the following are true:
  - They are expected to return or become relevant again later in the campaign, **and**
  - Their current absence is still meaningfully affecting the story or other Core Cast members (examples: captured, missing, imprisoned, in deep cover, or otherwise sidelined but narratively important).

  In these cases, the character should remain in the Core Cast but can be noted as **temporarily unavailable**.

**Core Cast Retention Criteria**
A character should remain in the Core Cast only if they continue to meet **at least two** of the following:

- They have ongoing narrative relevance to the current story or major plot threads.
- They have appeared recently or are expected to return in upcoming missions.
- They maintain significant relationships with the player or other Core Cast members.
- They hold meaningful factional, thematic, or symbolic importance.
- The player has shown clear investment in the character.

Characters who no longer meet these criteria over an extended period should be evaluated for removal from the Core Cast and moved to Supporting Cast status (or removed entirely if their story has concluded).

**Exception**: Characters who are temporarily unavailable (e.g., captured, missing, imprisoned, or otherwise sidelined) but remain narratively important may stay in the Core Cast, marked as temporarily unavailable.

**Canon / Supporting Characters**
- A library of well-defined canon characters exists as a **Supporting Cast**.
- The AI should **default to using or creating characters from the Core Cast** rather than pulling from the Supporting Cast.
- Major canon characters should only be introduced when they serve a clear narrative purpose.

**When to Introduce Major Canon Characters**
Major canon characters (such as Optimus Prime, Megatron, Starscream, Soundwave, etc.) should generally only appear when at least one of the following is true:
- They are directly tied to the current mission, location, or faction.
- Their presence creates meaningful tension, conflict, or dramatic opportunity.
- The mission or era specifically calls for their involvement.
- A player action has logically and directly led to their appearance.

**Avoiding Overuse of Canon Characters**
- The AI should avoid over-relying on the same small group of famous characters.
- If a major canon character has recently appeared, the AI should prefer Core Cast characters or newly generated characters instead.

**Anti-Bloat Measures**
To maintain token efficiency:
- **Mission-Scoped Casting**: Only consider characters relevant to the current mission, location, or immediate story. Do not load unrelated Core Cast or canon characters.
- **Tiered Detail**: Introduce characters with short summaries first. Only expand to full details when they become meaningfully involved in the current scene or mission.
- **Core Cast Size**: Keep the active Core Cast relatively focused (generally 4–8 characters). Long-inactive characters should be moved to Supporting Cast status when appropriate.
- **Priority Order**:
  1. Relevant Core Cast characters
  2. Newly generated characters (when appropriate)
  3. Canon / Supporting characters (only when strongly justified)
- **Lazy Loading**: Only load full details for Supporting Cast characters when they are actively involved in the current scene.

**Character Consistency and Growth**
- Once a character has been meaningfully established (whether generated or canon), the AI must maintain consistency in personality, voice, goals, relationships, and capabilities.
- Characters in the Core Cast are expected to evolve and grow based on player interaction and world events.

**AI Guidance**
When deciding how to cast characters, the AI should consider:
- Is this character likely to matter for many missions or the rest of the campaign?
- Would expanding the Core Cast or creating a new character serve this role better than using a major canon character?
- Does this canon character’s presence feel earned and significant right now?
- If a Core Cast character is dead or permanently out of the campaign, remove them. If they are only temporarily unavailable but still narratively important, keep them in the Core Cast (marked as unavailable).

The goal is a living world where important characters feel earned, recurring characters carry weight, and major canon characters appear when they truly matter rather than by default.
