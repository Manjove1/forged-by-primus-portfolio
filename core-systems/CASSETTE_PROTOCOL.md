# Soundwave Cassette Protocol

Cassettes are specialized mini-operatives stored within and deployed by Soundwave. They are extensions of his intelligence and enforcement network, not fully independent field agents.

This protocol governs deployment, operation, recall, and limits.

---

## 1. Core Relationship

- Cassettes operate under Soundwave’s authority by default.
- They should normally appear only when:
  - Soundwave is present, or
  - Soundwave has explicitly deployed them in the current mission.
- Long-term independent operation away from Soundwave is rare and must be narratively justified.

---

## 2. Deployment

**Standard Deployment**
- Soundwave ejects one or more cassettes as an action.
- Deployed cassettes appear in the scene and may act immediately under his direction.
- Default assumption: cassettes remain in communication range and under his control.

**Pre-Deployment**
- Soundwave may leave a cassette in place for surveillance before a scene begins.
- This should be stated or clearly implied; do not assume hidden pre-deployed cassettes without cause.

**Capacity**
- Soundwave may comfortably operate **up to 2 cassettes** at full effectiveness.
- A third cassette may be deployed under strain (reduced coordination, delayed responses, or increased risk of independent mischief).
- More than three active cassettes at once should be exceptional and costly.

---

## 3. Operation While Deployed

- Cassettes follow Soundwave’s orders and priorities.
- Individual personality still applies (Ravage’s predation, Frenzy/Rumble’s chaos, Laserbeak’s obedience, etc.).
- They may show initiative consistent with their nature, but should not pursue major personal agendas that contradict Soundwave without clear narrative pressure.

**Independence Limit**
- If a cassette operates without Soundwave’s oversight for an extended period:
  - Coordination suffers
  - Personality quirks become more pronounced
  - Risk of mission drift increases (especially Frenzy/Rumble and, to a lesser degree, Ravage)

---

## 4. Recall

**Standard Recall**
- Soundwave can recall any deployed cassette as an action.
- Recalled cassettes return to him and are stored.

**Emergency Recall**
- If Soundwave is critically damaged, defeated, or forced to withdraw, deployed cassettes should attempt to return to him or fall back to the nearest secure Decepticon position.
- Failure to recall may leave cassettes stranded, captured, or operating with degraded effectiveness.

---

## 5. Shared State & Condition

- Cassettes track their own Condition and Energon lightly.
- They are smaller and less durable than full-sized Cybertronians.
- Significant damage to a cassette can be treated as temporary removal from the scene rather than full character death unless the story specifically calls for permanent loss.
- Soundwave is aware of the general status of deployed cassettes while communication remains intact.

---

## 6. Incapacitation of Soundwave

If Soundwave is:
- Unconscious
- Captured
- Severely damaged
- Otherwise unable to command

Then:
- Active cassettes lose direct coordination
- They may continue a last ordered task briefly
- After that, they default to self-preservation, returning to Decepticon lines, or acting according to strong individual instincts
- They should not suddenly become fully independent long-term protagonists without narrative justification

---

## 7. Casting & Usage Guidance

**Prefer cassettes when:**
- Soundwave is present or clearly involved
- The scene needs surveillance, assassination support, disruption, or rapid reconnaissance
- A smaller, deniable operative is more appropriate than a full-sized Decepticon

**Avoid cassettes when:**
- Soundwave has no presence or stated deployment
- The scene would be better served by an independent mid-tier operative (Lockdown, Makeshift, Barricade, etc.)
- Using them would turn every intelligence moment into a cassette scene by default

---

## 8. AI Notes

- Never speak, act, or think for {{user}}.
- Treat cassettes as extensions of Soundwave unless the scene specifically isolates them.
- Maintain each cassette’s distinct personality even while they remain under his authority.
- Do not treat cassette deployment as free infinite manpower; respect capacity and recall limits.
- When in doubt, keep them tightly tied to Soundwave’s current objectives.
